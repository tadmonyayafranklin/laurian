Research and publications

Ph.D Thesis

Title: Efficient computation and security of pairings for cryptography.

Supervisors: FOUOTSA Emmanuel, Associate Professor; AYISSI Raoul, Professor

Abstract: Numerous cryptosystems based on pairings such as identity-based encryption and signature schemes involve hashing to second elliptic curve subgroup G2 . Hashing to G2 is in general costly. The first contribution of this thesis is an improvement to the Fuentes et al. method for hashing into G2 on the recommended Aurefeuillan family of elliptic curves at higher embedding degree. This is done by providing matrices with rational coefficients to which the application of the LLL algorithm was infeasible for high embedding degrees. The second contribution of this thesis is done by establishing novel formulas suitable for the parallel computation for the β-Weil pairing on curves with odd emebdding degrees having twists of degree 3. The last result of this work is an improvement of the existing superoptimal pairing by eliminating some extra exponentiations that heavily affect the efficiency of this pairing. This led to the introduction of a new pairing called x-superoptimal for which the Miller loop is about 27.3% and 49% faster than the one of the optimal Ate pairing previously computed on BW 13 − P 310 and BW 19 − P 286 respectively. All over this work, the correctness of all formulas derived has been verified also by the computer algebra softwares Magma or SageMaths.

M.sc Thesis

Title: The algorithm of computing discrete logarithms and the impact on the security of the cryptographic protocols based on pairings.

Supervisors: FOUOTSA Emmanuel, Associate Professor; Pola Emmanuel , Senior Lecture

Abstract: Amongst the difficult mathematical problems that enable us to build robust and secured crypto-systems is that of computing the discrete logarithm in the multiplicative group of a finite field or in the group of rational points of an elliptic curve. The index calculus is one of algorithms used to solve this problem in the multiplicative group of a finite field, but it still under sub-exponential complexity. In the case of the elliptic curves the algorithm of Pholig-Hellman tries to solve the same problem with exponential complexity. But the pairing, bilinear applications defined on elliptic curves, make it possible to transfer the problem towards the multiplicative groups. For a certain time now, number field sieve algorithm (NFS), inspired by the index calculus algorithm, knows significant improvements and therefore threatens the security of systems based on discrete logarithm. The objective of this thesis is to present the various phases of the NFS in the field of large and medium characteristics as well as an analysis of complexities. We will finish by analyzing the impact of these complexities on the pairing based protocols.
